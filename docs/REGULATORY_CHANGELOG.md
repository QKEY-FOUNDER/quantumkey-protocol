## 0. Purpose

This document records **regulatory developments** relevant to the QuantumKey Protocol (QKP)
and documents how the project monitors, assesses, and responds to regulatory change.

Its purpose is to:
- demonstrate continuous regulatory awareness
- support audit, banking, and partner due diligence
- prevent silent regulatory drift

This document does not introduce new obligations.
It records awareness and assessment.

---

## 1. Regulatory Baseline

The regulatory baseline for QKP is defined by:

- EU Markets in Crypto-Assets Regulation (EU) 2023/1114 (MiCA)
- Implementing Regulation (EU) 2025/304
- RTS/ITS published by ESMA (February 2025)
- National implementation laws (e.g. Portugal – Law No. 69/2025)

The project’s regulatory position is defined in:
- `docs/REGULATORY_POSITION_MiCA.md`
- `docs/MICA_TOKEN_CLASSIFICATION.md`

---

## 2. Monitoring Scope

The QKP project actively monitors:

- MiCA delegated and implementing acts
- ESMA regulatory technical standards (RTS)
- ESMA guidelines and Q&A
- National supervisory guidance (Banco de Portugal, CMVM)
- Relevant court or enforcement actions affecting crypto-asset classification

**Source:** ESMA / Official Journal of the European Union  

**Assessment:**  
- QKP is a protocol specification, not an issuer or CASP.

**Action Taken:**

### 2025-03

**Event:** Implementing Regulation (EU) 2025/304 enters into force  
**Source:** EUR-Lex  

**Assessment:**  
- Templates apply to notification and authorization procedures.  
- QKP does not submit notifications or operate services.  

**Action Taken:**  
- No action required  
- Documented non-applicability

**Event:** Portuguese Law No. 69/2025 enters into force  
**Source:** Diário da República  

**Assessment:**  
- Defines competent authorities (Banco de Portugal / CMVM).  
- Applies to issuers and service providers only.  

**Action Taken:**  
- Jurisdictional analysis added to regulatory position documents
